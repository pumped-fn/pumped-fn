# Research

## Purpose

`research/` holds accepted research notes that inform current package and workflow decisions.

## Structure

| Directory | Role |
| --- | --- |
| `learnings/` | Evidence-backed learnings kept for future implementation and review work. |

## Naming

Research files use short kebab-case names that identify the surface and finding. Include dates in
the content when timing or measured results matter.

## Content Rules

Keep research source-grounded. Notes should name evidence, decision impact, open questions, and
whether the finding is accepted or still candidate.

## Boundaries

Do not store scratch transcripts, generated caches, or unratified implementation plans here. If a
learning becomes package behavior, move the durable contract into the relevant README, PATTERNS file,
or tests.
